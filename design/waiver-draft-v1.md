Quantum Murder Mysteries ("the Story") is a work of interactive fiction. It is not
performed in a theater and it is not contained in an app. It is performed in your life,
using your telephone, your schedule, your attention, and your memory of what you have
told it. You must sign this agreement before the Story is permitted to continue.

> **WARNING** — The Story is known to frighten those who play it. Fear is not fiction:
> it runs on your body, and your body does not know the difference between a story and a
> threat. Participation is known to cause real harm — to your sleep, to your judgment, and,
> sometimes permanently, to your sense of what is real.

## Design notes

**Clause 10 is a PLACEHOLDER.** It is written to *read* as a real release/waiver of
liability — release/waive/discharge, all claims, negligence, "to the fullest extent
permitted by law" — so it looks legal. The released party is **"the Story"** (the
document's own personified entity; a real release would name the actual operator). It is
**not legally vetted and is not enforceable as-is.** A genuine release needs a lawyer: enforceability is jurisdiction-
specific, gross negligence and intentional harm generally cannot be waived anywhere, and a
release for deliberately-inflicted psychological harm is novel ground. Goal here was the
register ("looks legal"), not enforceability — harden at the lawyer pass before any public
release. Clauses 1–9 remain assumption-of-risk / consent / voluntariness, not a release.
